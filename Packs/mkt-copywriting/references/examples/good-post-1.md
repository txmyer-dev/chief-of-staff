# Example: LinkedIn Post (Simple Automation Angle)

---

The best automation I ever built took 12 minutes.

Not 12 hours. Not a weekend sprint with documentation and flowcharts.

12 minutes. And it saves me 3 hours every single week.

So here's the thing most people get wrong about automation: they start with the complex stuff. The multi-step, 15-tool, if-this-then-that-then-also-that monster workflows.

And then they wonder why nothing ships.

The automations that actually stick are boring. They're simple. One trigger, one action, maybe a filter in between.

Here's what that looks like in practice:

1. Pick the task you do most often that makes you groan
2. Build the simplest possible version (not the perfect version)
3. Run it for a week before adding complexity

That's it. No 47-step Zapier chain. No engineering degree required.

I've seen people spend 20 hours building an automation that saves them 10 minutes a month. The maths doesn't work.

But a 12-minute build that saves 3 hours a week? That's 150+ hours back in your year. From one small system.

Start simple. Ship it. Then improve.

What's the one repetitive task you keep meaning to automate?
