# Variant Generation and Quality Scoring

Never ship the first version. Generate options, measure quality, then recommend tests.

---

## Headline Variants

Produce at least 5 headlines per project, each using a different angle from the persuasion toolkit:

1. **Outcome-first** — plainest possible statement of the benefit. No cleverness. Maximum clarity.
2. **Curiosity** — implies a discovery or insight without revealing it. Specific enough to seem real.
3. **Social proof** — opens with a number, a name, or a collective result.
4. **Contrarian** — challenges a widely held assumption. Must be genuinely surprising.
5. **Story** — implies a narrative arc. Setup → tension → implied resolution.

Generate 2-5 more by combining angles: question + outcome, data + transformation, how-to + time constraint, warning + curiosity, timely hook + outcome.

### How to present them

Open with a quick recommendation so the user can grab and go:

```
TOP PICK
★ "{Recommended headline}"
  → Suited for: {audience awareness level}
  → Rationale: {one sentence}

All {N} options below.
```

Then the numbered list. Star the recommended one.

## Body Copy Variants

For landing pages and sales pages, produce at least 2 complete drafts from different entry points:

### Draft A: Primary angle

The most straightforward version of the strongest positioning. Uses whatever framework best fits the format — full landing page arc for pages, hook-value-CTA for emails, hook-benefit-CTA for ads. This is the one you'd ship if budget only allowed one test.

### Draft B: Challenge angle

Enters through a counterintuitive or provocative take. Opens with something that makes the reader pause and reconsider. The body reframes the problem from an unexpected direction. Same offer, different door.

Works best with sceptical audiences, saturated categories, or brand voices that lean edgy.

### Draft C: Evidence-first

Skips the buildup entirely and leads with the single strongest proof point. No warm-up. The reader sees a result before they see the pitch.

Structure: strongest proof → context (who, how, when) → bridge to offer → additional evidence → CTA.

Works best when the audience already knows what the product is and just needs convincing that it actually delivers.

### Variant metadata

After each draft, include:

```
Angle: [Primary / Challenge / Evidence-first]
Best audience: [awareness level, market condition]
Voice match: [aligned with voice profile / adjusted for this angle]
Test recommendation: [what to compare it against and why]
```

## Email Subject Lines

When writing emails, produce 5-7 subject line options across different approaches:

1. Outcome-focused
2. Curiosity-driven
3. Story teaser
4. Direct question
5. Contrarian/unexpected
6. (If applicable) Personalised using audience data
7. (If applicable) Time-sensitive — only when the urgency is real

Star the recommendation.

## Quality Scoring

Seven dimensions, each rated 1-10:

| # | Dimension | The question it answers | A 10 means... |
|---|-----------|------------------------|---------------|
| 1 | Clarity | Could someone with no context get the point immediately? | Zero re-reading needed. A child could summarise it. |
| 2 | Specificity | Are claims backed by concrete numbers or examples? | Every assertion has evidence attached. |
| 3 | Voice | Does it sound like a particular person wrote this? | Distinctive, human, couldn't be anyone else. |
| 4 | Desire | Does the reader actively want the thing by mid-page? | Visceral pull toward the CTA. They feel what they're missing. |
| 5 | Proof | Is there evidence the claims are true? | Multiple data points, named sources, verifiable outcomes. |
| 6 | Urgency | Is there a genuine reason to act now rather than later? | Real deadline or consequence of waiting. Not manufactured. |
| 7 | Flow | Does each section pull naturally into the next? | Couldn't stop reading if they tried. |

### Presenting scores

```
QUALITY SCORECARD

Clarity:      8/10   "Offer is clear, mechanism needs more detail"
Specificity:  6/10   "Too many round numbers, not enough named sources"
Voice:        7/10   "Reads well but could belong to any brand in this space"
Desire:       5/10   "Benefits listed but not felt — needs scenarios"
Proof:        4/10   "Single testimonial, no metrics, no case study"
Urgency:      3/10   "No reason to act this week vs next quarter"
Flow:         7/10   "Smooth overall, section 3 is a wall of text"

TOTAL:       40/70   (57%)

Verdict: Below threshold. Rewrite the weakest dimensions.

Priority fixes:
1. Add 2-3 outcome-specific testimonials (Proof: 4 → 7)
2. Make the pain concrete with numbers (Desire: 5 → 8)
3. Add real scarcity or a deadline (Urgency: 3 → 6)
```

### Thresholds

| Score | Range | Action |
|-------|-------|--------|
| 63-70 | 90-100% | Ship. Minor polish at most. |
| 56-62 | 80-89% | Strong. Ship with the noted tweaks. |
| 49-55 | 70-79% | Functional but underperforming. Strengthen weak dimensions. |
| 42-48 | 60-69% | Needs targeted rewriting before publishing. |
| Below 42 | Under 60% | Fundamental issues. Full rewrite. |

### When to run scoring

- **Before rewriting existing copy** — score first, show gaps, then rewrite. Builds trust.
- **After generating new copy** — self-assess honestly. Flag any dimension under 7 and offer to fix it.
- **On request** — whenever the user asks to evaluate or rate a piece of copy.

## Split Test Recommendations

After generating copy, suggest 3-5 testable changes with this format:

```
Test [N]: [What's changing] — [Option A] vs [Option B]
Hypothesis: [Why one might outperform, grounded in a principle]
Measure:    [The specific metric: CTR, conversion, engagement, time on page]
Expected:   [Directional estimate: +X-Y% on the metric]
Priority:   [HIGH / MEDIUM / LOW]
```

### What to test, in order

**HIGH priority:**
1. **Headlines** — the element with the most leverage. Different headline angles can produce wildly different results. Test first, always.
2. **Opening hook** — determines whether they read past the fold. Test different entry styles.
3. **CTA wording and placement** — benefit-oriented vs direct command, position above vs below proof section.

**MEDIUM priority:**
4. **Evidence structure** — lead with a testimonial vs lead with data vs lead with the founder's story.
5. **Copy length** — shorter for highly aware audiences, longer for less aware. The right length depends on how much context the reader needs.

**LOW priority (optimise once the above are settled):**
6. **Body angle** — primary vs challenge vs evidence-first from the variants above.
7. **Friction reducers** — the guarantee copy, social proof numbers, and ease-of-start claims below the CTA.
