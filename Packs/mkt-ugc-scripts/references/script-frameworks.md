# Script Frameworks

Proven structures for short-form talking-head video. Each framework includes the structure, timing breakdown, when to use it, and an example skeleton.

Pick the framework that matches the topic type — don't force a story into a list format or a hot take into a tutorial.

---

## Quick Teach (15-30s)

**When to use:** You're teaching one specific thing. A tip, a hack, a technique. The viewer should be able to do something new after watching.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — Bold claim or call out your audience directly
[3-20s] DELIVER — 3-5 points, rapid-fire. One sentence per point.
[20-30s] CLOSE — "Save this" / "Follow for more" / loop back to hook
```

**What makes it work:** Speed and specificity. Don't explain why — just show what. The audience saves it to come back to later.

**Example skeleton:**
```
[0-3s] [emphasis] "Stop doing X the hard way."
[ON SCREEN: "The X shortcut nobody uses"]
[3-8s] "Here's what to do instead."
[8-20s] [speed up] "Step one — [action]. Step two — [action]. Step three — [action]."
[ON SCREEN: Steps listed as they're spoken]
[20-30s] "Save this before you forget it."
```

## Story Arc (30-60s)

**When to use:** You have a personal experience, a client result, or a lesson learned. Something happened, and it changed how you think or work.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — Drop in mid-story. No setup, no "So today I want to talk about..."
[3-10s] CONTEXT — Quick setup. Who, what, where. 2-3 sentences max.
[10-40s] TENSION — The problem, the struggle, the unexpected thing. Build it.
[40-55s] PAYOFF — The lesson, the result, the shift. Make it specific.
[55-60s] CLOSE — Question to the audience or loop back to the hook.
```

**What makes it work:** Tension. If there's no moment where the viewer thinks "wait, what happened?", the story is flat. Drop the most surprising part into the hook.

**Example skeleton:**
```
[0-3s] [lean in] "So I lost $X doing something I thought was smart."
[3-10s] "Last [month/year], I was [situation]. Everything was going great."
[10-25s] "Then [the problem]. And here's what made it worse — [detail]."
[25-40s] [slow down] "What I didn't realise was [the insight]."
[40-55s] "So now I [what changed]. And the result? [specific outcome]."
[55-60s] "Has this happened to you? Drop a comment."
```

## Myth Bust (15-45s)

**When to use:** There's a common belief in your industry that's wrong, outdated, or oversimplified. You're going to correct it.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — "Stop believing [myth]" or "Everyone says [thing]. They're wrong."
[3-25s] WHY IT'S WRONG — Evidence, logic, or experience. Be specific.
[25-40s] WHAT'S ACTUALLY TRUE — The correct take, with proof if possible.
[40-45s] CLOSE — "Do this instead" or "Share this with someone who needs it"
```

**What makes it work:** The strength of the myth. If nobody actually believes it, there's nothing to bust. Pick beliefs your audience holds right now.

**Example skeleton:**
```
[0-3s] [emphasis] "You don't need X to Y. Here's why."
[ON SCREEN: "THE X MYTH"]
[3-15s] "Everyone says [myth]. I believed it too for [time period]."
[15-25s] "But here's what actually happens — [evidence/experience]."
[25-40s] [lean in] "What works instead: [alternative]. I've seen [result] doing this."
[40-45s] "Share this with someone still stuck on [myth]."
```

## Hot Take / Reaction (15-30s)

**When to use:** You have a strong opinion about something happening in your industry. Something's trending, something changed, or something popular is actually bad.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — The take itself, stated boldly. No buildup.
[3-20s] BACK IT UP — Why you believe this. 2-3 specific reasons.
[20-30s] CLOSE — Challenge the audience to agree/disagree. Bait the comments.
```

**What makes it work:** Conviction. If you hedge the take, it's not a take. Be willing to be wrong and to have people disagree. The comments section is the point.

**Example skeleton:**
```
[0-3s] [emphasis] "[Bold opinion]. I'll die on this hill."
[ON SCREEN: The hot take as text]
[3-10s] "Here's why — [reason one]."
[10-20s] "And [reason two]. Look at [evidence]."
[20-30s] "Tell me I'm wrong. I'll wait."
```

## Problem-Agitate-Solve (30-45s)

**When to use:** You're selling something without being salesy. You name a problem your audience has, make them feel it, then present your solution as the obvious answer.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — Name the problem. Call out who has it.
[3-15s] AGITATE — Make the pain specific. What does it cost them? What have they tried?
[15-35s] SOLVE — Present the solution. Show the result. Keep it concrete.
[35-45s] CLOSE — Soft CTA. "Link in bio" / "DM me [word]" / "Follow for more"
```

**What makes it work:** The agitate section. If you skip straight from problem to solution, it feels like an ad. Spend time in the pain — the viewer needs to feel seen before they'll trust your answer.

**Example skeleton:**
```
[0-3s] "If you're a [role] still doing [painful thing], this is for you."
[3-8s] [lean in] "You've probably tried [common solution]. Didn't work, right?"
[8-15s] "That's because [reason it doesn't work]. And it's costing you [specific cost]."
[15-30s] "Here's what actually works — [solution]. [Specific proof/result]."
[ON SCREEN: Before/after or result metric]
[30-45s] "I put a free [resource] in my bio. Go grab it."
```

## Before/After (15-30s)

**When to use:** You have a transformation to show — a result, a redesign, a process improvement. The contrast tells the story.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — Show or state the "after" first. Lead with the result.
[3-10s] BEFORE — What it looked like before. Make it relatable.
[10-25s] THE SHIFT — What changed. Be specific about the method.
[25-30s] CLOSE — "Want me to show you how?" / "Save this"
```

**What makes it work:** Starting with the result. If you start with "before", people don't know what they're waiting for. Flash the payoff, then explain.

## "I Tried X" Review (30-60s)

**When to use:** You've tested a product, strategy, tool, or approach. You're sharing an honest verdict. Works well for building trust and authority.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — "I tried [thing] for [time period]. Here's what happened."
[3-15s] CONTEXT — What it is, why you tried it, what you expected
[15-40s] THE VERDICT — What worked, what didn't. Be specific and honest.
[40-55s] WHO IT'S FOR — "This is great for [persona], not great for [persona]"
[55-60s] CLOSE — "Have you tried it? What was your experience?"
```

**What makes it work:** Honesty. If everything is positive, it sounds sponsored. Include at least one genuine downside or surprise.

## Day-in-My-Life / POV (15-30s)

**When to use:** You're building relatability and showing what life in your role/industry actually looks like. Great for humanising a brand.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — "POV: You're a [role] and [relatable scenario]"
[3-20s] THE SCENARIO — Walk through the experience. Specific details matter.
[20-30s] CLOSE — Punchline, lesson, or "tell me this isn't just me"
```

**What makes it work:** Specificity. "POV: you're a marketer" is boring. "POV: you're a marketer and the client just asked to 'make the logo bigger' for the third time" — that hits.

## Testimonial / Social Proof (30-45s)

**When to use:** You're sharing a client or customer result. The script is written from your perspective about their experience, or structured as a guided testimonial.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — The result, stated plainly. "[Client type] went from [before] to [after]"
[3-15s] THE SITUATION — Who they were, what they were struggling with
[15-30s] WHAT CHANGED — The specific thing they did differently
[30-40s] THE RESULT — Numbers, outcomes, quotes. Make it concrete.
[40-45s] CLOSE — "If you're in [same situation], [soft CTA]"
```

**What makes it work:** The specificity of the result. "They grew their business" means nothing. "They went from $3k to $18k months in 90 days" — that's a story.

---

## Listicle / Rapid Fire (15-30s)

**When to use:** You have 3-7 items to share — tools, mistakes, tips, resources. Quick and punchy.

```
[0-3s] HOOK — "[Number] [things] every [role] needs to know"
[3-25s] THE LIST — One item per beat. 3-5 seconds each. No explanations, just items.
[25-30s] CLOSE — "Which one surprised you?" / "Save this list"
```

**What makes it work:** Speed and unexpected items. If everything on the list is obvious, nobody saves it. Include at least one surprise.

## Duration Guidelines

All scripts cap at 90 seconds. Most should land between 15-60s.

| Target | Word count | Best formats |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| 15 seconds | 30-40 words | Hot Take, Quick Teach, Listicle |
| 30 seconds | 65-80 words | Quick Teach, Myth Bust, Before/After, PAS |
| 45 seconds | 100-120 words | Story Arc, PAS, "I Tried X", Testimonial |
| 60 seconds | 140-160 words | Story Arc, "I Tried X", Testimonial |
| 90 seconds (max) | 200-225 words | Extended Story Arc, deep "I Tried X", detailed Testimonial |

These are approximate — natural speech varies. Some people talk faster. Delivery cues like [pause] and [slow down] eat into the word count.

## Platform Adjustments

The framework stays the same across platforms. What changes:

| | TikTok | Instagram Reels | YouTube Shorts |
|--|--------|----------------|----------------|
| **Tone** | Raw, unpolished, "figured this out" energy | Slightly more polished, still casual | Informational, "here to help" energy |
| **Hook style** | Pattern interrupt, stir the pot | Curiosity, visual-first | Bold claim, show the result |
| **Trending sounds** | Use them (even at low volume) | Less important since 2025 | Not applicable |
| **CTA** | "Follow for part 2" / stitch bait | "Save this" / "Share with a friend" | "Subscribe" / "Watch the full video" |
| **Captions** | Non-negotiable | Non-negotiable | Strongly recommended |
| **Length sweet spot** | 15-30s for reach, 30-60s for depth | 7-15s for reach, 30-45s for teaching | 15-35s |
