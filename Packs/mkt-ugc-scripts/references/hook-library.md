# Hook Library

Hooks for talking-head delivery. These are designed to be spoken to camera — they work because of how they sound, not just how they read. Every hook is meant to stop the scroll in the first 3 seconds.

Pick hooks that match the script format and the speaker's natural style. Forced hooks feel worse than no hook at all.

---

## Identity Call-Out

Name the audience directly. People stop scrolling when they hear themselves described.

- "If you're a [role] who [specific situation], stop scrolling."
- "This is for the [role] who's been told to [common advice] but it's not working."
- "Only [role]s will understand why this matters."
- "You're probably a [role] if [relatable habit]."
- "Every [role] I know is dealing with this right now."

**Best for:** Quick Teach, PAS, Myth Bust
**Delivery:** Direct to camera, confident. No smiling. This is serious.

---

## Pattern Interrupt

Start mid-thought. No intro, no "hey guys", no context. Drop the viewer into something unexpected.

- "So here's what nobody tells you about [topic]..." (start mid-sentence)
- "I wasn't going to share this, but..."
- "This is the part where I'm supposed to [expected thing]. I'm not going to."
- "Okay, stop what you're doing." [pause] "This is important."
- "[Do something unusual on camera — hold up an object, point at something, start walking]"

**Best for:** Hot Take, Story Arc, Myth Bust
**Delivery:** Casual, almost like you're continuing a conversation they walked into.

---

## Contrarian / Pot-Stirring

Challenge something the audience believes. Make them want to defend their position (or change it).

- "[Popular opinion] is completely wrong. Here's why."
- "I'm going to get hate for this, but [take]."
- "Unpopular opinion: [take]. And I have receipts."
- "Everyone's saying [trend]. Nobody's talking about the downside."
- "The advice you keep hearing about [topic]? It's outdated."
- "I stopped doing [common practice] and here's what happened."

**Best for:** Hot Take, Myth Bust, "I Tried X"
**Delivery:** Calm confidence. Not angry, not ranty. The calmer you are with a hot take, the more it hits.

---

## Result / Outcome First

Lead with what happened. The "how" is why they keep watching.

- "This one change made me [specific result]."
- "[Client type] went from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]."
- "I did [thing] for [time period]. Here are the numbers."
- "This costs $0 and took me 10 minutes. The result? [outcome]."
- "Before: [state]. After: [state]. Here's the only thing I changed."

**Best for:** Before/After, Testimonial, "I Tried X", Quick Teach
**Delivery:** Start with the result stated plainly. Let the result do the work — don't oversell it.

---

## Curiosity Gap

Open a question the viewer needs answered. The gap between what they know and what you're about to tell them is what keeps them watching.

- "There's a reason [thing] stopped working in [year]. Nobody's talking about it."
- "The thing about [topic] that nobody is willing to say out loud..."
- "I found out why [surprising thing]. It's not what you think."
- "What would you do if [scenario]? Because that happened to me."
- "Here's what [authority figure] does differently — and it's not [obvious thing]."

**Best for:** Story Arc, Myth Bust, "I Tried X"
**Delivery:** Slightly lower energy. Draw them in, don't shout at them.

---

## Direct Challenge

Command attention. Tell the viewer what to do or what they're doing wrong.

- "Stop [common mistake]. Right now. Here's what to do instead."
- "If you're still [outdated practice] in [year], we need to talk."
- "Delete [thing] from your [system]. It's hurting you."
- "You're spending [time/money] on [thing] and it's not working. I know because I did too."
- "Raise your hand if [relatable problem]. Yeah. Let me fix that."

**Best for:** Quick Teach, PAS, Myth Bust
**Delivery:** Firm but helpful. You're the friend who tells them the truth, not a drill sergeant.

---

## "I Tried" / Confession

Share something you tested, tried, or learned the hard way. Vulnerability builds trust fast.

- "I tried [thing] for [period]. Here's my honest review."
- "I spent [money/time] on [thing] so you don't have to."
- "I was wrong about [topic]. Here's what changed my mind."
- "I used to tell my clients to [advice]. I stopped. Here's why."
- "This is embarrassing, but I [mistake]. And I learned something."

**Best for:** "I Tried X", Story Arc, Hot Take
**Delivery:** Genuine. Not performed vulnerability — actual honesty. People can tell the difference.

---

## Timeliness / Urgency

Tie the hook to something happening right now. Trending topics, algorithm changes, new releases, seasonal moments.

- "[Platform] just changed [thing]. Here's what it means for you."
- "If you haven't heard about [trend] yet, you're already behind."
- "[Month/event] is coming. Here's what smart [roles] are doing right now."
- "This just happened in [industry] and nobody's covering it."
- "I predicted [thing] would happen. It just did. Here's what's next."

**Best for:** Hot Take, Quick Teach, News Reaction
**Delivery:** Energised but not breathless. Urgency comes from the information, not from shouting.

---

## Choosing the Right Hook

| Script format | Best hook categories |
|---------------|---------------------|
| Quick Teach | Identity Call-Out, Direct Challenge, Result First |
| Story Arc | Pattern Interrupt, Curiosity Gap, "I Tried" |
| Myth Bust | Contrarian, Direct Challenge, Curiosity Gap |
| Hot Take | Contrarian, Pattern Interrupt, Timeliness |
| PAS | Identity Call-Out, Direct Challenge, Result First |
| Before/After | Result First, "I Tried", Curiosity Gap |
| "I Tried X" | "I Tried", Result First, Curiosity Gap |
| Day-in-My-Life | Pattern Interrupt, Identity Call-Out |
| Testimonial | Result First, "I Tried" |
| Listicle | Identity Call-Out, Direct Challenge |

---

## Hook Testing

When writing batch scripts, vary the hook category across the batch. If all 5 scripts use identity call-outs, the content feels repetitive. Mix it up:
- Script 1: Identity Call-Out + Quick Teach
- Script 2: Contrarian + Hot Take
- Script 3: Result First + Before/After
- Script 4: Curiosity Gap + Story Arc
- Script 5: Direct Challenge + PAS
