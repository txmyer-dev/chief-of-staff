# Text Platforms: LinkedIn + Twitter/X

What actually works on each platform right now. Format specs, what the algorithm rewards, post structures that perform, and hooks worth using.

---

## LinkedIn

### What the Algorithm Cares About

- **Dwell time** is still king. The longer someone stays on your post, the more it gets pushed. Carousels and long-text posts win here.
- **Topic consistency** builds trust. Post about the same niche regularly and LinkedIn treats you as an authority on it.
- **The first hour matters most.** Strong engagement in the first 60 minutes determines whether your post reaches beyond your immediate network.
- **Old posts still get shown** if they're relevant. Since mid-2025, LinkedIn resurfaces posts from 2-3 weeks ago if they match what someone's interested in.
- **Engagement pods get caught.** LinkedIn's AI detects coordinated commenting patterns. Don't bother.
- **Links kill reach.** Posts with external URLs get suppressed. Put links in comments instead.

Worth knowing: organic reach is down roughly 50% vs a year ago. Quality over quantity. 2-3 posts per week is plenty.

### Format Specs

| Format | Details | Why |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Carousel | 5-10 slides, 1080x1350px | Highest dwell time. People save these. |
| Text post | 1,200-1,500 chars | Sweet spot for depth without losing readers |
| Document | PDF, 10-15 pages | Works well for step-by-step frameworks |
| Video | 30-90 sec, needs captions | Gets less reach than text or carousels |

### How to Structure a Carousel

- **Slide 1:** A bold claim or question that makes them stop scrolling. Minimal design, high contrast. End with "Swipe to see [outcome]."
- **Slides 2-6:** One idea per slide. Number it. Give a headline and 2-3 sentences. Keep text readable on a phone — 24pt minimum, 40pt+ for headlines.
- **Second-to-last slide:** Recap everything in 5 words per point. People who swiped fast catch up here.
- **Final slide:** CTA. "Follow for more [topic]." "Repost if useful." "Save this."

### How to Structure a Text Post

```
[First line that stops the scroll — this is 90% of the work]

[2-3 lines of context — why this matters right now]

Here's what I've found:

1. [Point + why it matters]
2. [Point + why it matters]
3. [Point + why it matters]

[The takeaway — one sentence, make it land]

[Question or CTA that invites a response]

---
[3-5 hashtags, bottom of the post, separated by a line break]
```

### Hooks That Work

1. **Challenge the norm:** "Stop [common practice]. It's costing you [result]."
2. **Open a story:** "Last week I [thing]. What happened changed how I see [topic]."
3. **Tease a list:** "[Number] [things] that [outcome]. Number [X] is the one nobody talks about."
4. **Lead with proof:** "After [experience/stat], here's what I know for sure about [topic]:"
5. **Ask a real question:** "Why does [surprising thing] keep happening? I think I've worked it out."
6. **Make a claim:** "[Counterintuitive statement]. Here's the proof."

### Don't Do This

1. Putting links in the post body (use first comment)
2. More than 5 hashtags
3. No line breaks (wall of text = scroll past)
4. Twitter energy on LinkedIn (too casual, wrong audience)

## Twitter/X

### What the Algorithm Cares About

- **Replies beat everything.** Especially replies from accounts you already interact with. This is weighted highest.
- **Quote tweets are worth 2x** a plain retweet. Adding your take to someone else's post gets rewarded.
- **Time spent on your tweet** matters — reading time, link clicks, everything.
- **Profile clicks** signal curiosity. Tweets that make people check your profile get boosted.
- **Images and video increase visibility.** Not massively, but enough to matter.
- **First few hours decide everything.** Early engagement triggers amplification. Late engagement does little.

Since 2025: the Following feed uses Grok AI for ranking — it's no longer chronological. Most users land on the For You feed by default.

### Format Specs

| Format | Details | Why |
|--------|---------|-----|
| Single tweet | Under 100 chars is ideal | Highest engagement per impression |
| Thread | 8-15 tweets | Best for building followers and showing depth |
| Quote tweet | Must add real value | 2x engagement vs plain retweet |
| Image tweet | 1200x675px | ~35% more engagement than text-only |

### How to Structure a Thread

**Tweet 1 — the hook decides everything:**
```
[Bold statement or specific promise]

[One line that tells them what they'll get]

Thread:
```

**Tweets 2 through N — each one earns the next:**
```
[Number]. [Point in one line]

[2-3 sentences explaining why this matters]

[Proof, example, or result if you have one]
```

**Last tweet — wrap it up and ask for the share:**
```
TL;DR:
- [Key point]
- [Key point]
- [Key point]

If this helped:
1. Follow @[handle]
2. RT tweet 1

[Link if you've got one]
```

### Single Tweet Formats

**The flip:** "[What most people think]. But [what's actually true], because [reason]."

**The list:** "[Number] [things] that [outcome]: [bullets]. Which one hits?"

**The take:** "Unpopular opinion: [claim]. Here's why: [one sentence]."

**The receipt:** "[Result you got]. Here's exactly how: [3-5 steps]."

### Hooks That Work

1. **Declare something dead:** "[Thing] is dead. Here's what's replacing it:"
2. **Show your work:** "I [did thing] for [time period]. Here's what happened:"
3. **Pick a fight:** "This will annoy [group], but [claim]."
4. **Open a gap:** "The [industry] secret nobody talks about:"
5. **Lead with receipts:** "[Result] in [timeframe]. No [common excuse]. Here's the playbook:"

### Don't Do This

1. Threads longer than 15-20 tweets (people drop off)
2. A weak first tweet (nothing else matters if tweet 1 doesn't land)
3. Filler tweets that pad the count but add nothing
4. Ignoring replies (the algorithm watches this)
5. Posting when your audience is asleep
