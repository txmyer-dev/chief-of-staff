# Visual + Video Platforms: Instagram + TikTok + YouTube

What drives results on visual and video-first platforms. Specs, what the algorithm actually rewards, structures that work, and common traps.

---

## Instagram

### What the Algorithm Cares About

- **DM shares are massive.** "Sends per reach" is now one of the strongest signals for Reels and Explore. Make content people want to forward to a friend.
- **Saves still matter.** A save tells Instagram this content has lasting value. Huge for Feed and Explore placement.
- **Reel completion rate is everything.** For Reels, how much of the video people watch is the primary ranking signal.
- **Quality of likes, not quantity.** Instagram looks at likes-per-reach, not total likes. A post with 50 likes from 200 views beats 200 likes from 10,000 views.
- **First 30-90 minutes set the ceiling.** Early engagement determines how far your content gets pushed.
- **Relationships compound.** If someone DMs you, visits your profile, and comments regularly, Instagram shows them more of your stuff.

Since 2025: photos are getting love again (not just video). Carousels can go up to 20 slides. Hashtags matter way less than they used to — content signals dominate.

### Format Specs

| Format | Details | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Carousel | 6-10 slides (up to 20), 1080x1350px | Engagement + saves. Drives the most action. |
| Reel | 7-15 sec for reach, 30-45 sec for teaching | Discovery. How new people find you. |
| Single image | 1080x1350px | Getting supported again in 2025 |
| Story | 1080x1920px, under 15 sec | DM replies and close-audience engagement |

### How to Build a Carousel

- **Slide 1:** Stop the scroll. Bold text, one idea, high contrast. Create curiosity.
- **Slide 2:** Set up the problem. "Here's what most people get wrong" or "The thing nobody tells you."
- **Slides 3-7:** One point per slide. Keep it readable — if someone can't get it from a quick glance on their phone, simplify.
- **Slides 8-9:** Recap for the fast-swipers.
- **Slide 10:** CTA. Save, follow, share.

Design: 4:5 ratio (1080x1350px). Minimum 24pt body text, 40pt+ headlines. High contrast always.

### How to Script a Reel

```
[0-3 sec] HOOK — say something that makes them stay. Start mid-thought, not with an intro.
[3-25 sec] DELIVER — 3-5 points, or a story with tension. Keep it moving.
[25-30 sec] CLOSE — "Save this" / "Follow for more" / loop the ending back to the start.
```

Two-thirds of viewers decide whether to keep watching in the first 3 seconds. Front-load the hook — visually and verbally, at the same time.

### Reel Formats That Work

1. **Talking head + text on screen** — you speaking with key points overlaid. Good for establishing authority.
2. **Before/after** — show the transformation immediately. Works for anything results-based.
3. **POV setup** — "POV: You just [relatable scenario]." Good for humour and relatability.
4. **Quick tutorial** — show the process, no fluff. Best for actionable stuff.
5. **Trending audio + your angle** — ride the wave of a trending sound, twist it to your niche.

### Caption Structures

**Tell a story:** "[Hook] → When I [setup]... → [The struggle] → Then [turning point] → [Lesson] → What's yours?"

**Make a list:** "[Number + benefit hook] → Here's what I mean: → 1. [Point] → 2. [Point] → 3. [Point] → Which one?"

**Teach something:** "[Contrarian opener] → What people miss: → [Explanation] → Do this instead: [Steps] → Save for later"

### Don't Do This

1. Only posting static images (the algorithm deprioritises them unless they're genuinely good)
2. Video without captions (80%+ of people watch on mute)
3. Reposting TikToks with the watermark still on
4. Random visual style — inconsistency makes your grid look like a mess

## TikTok

### What the Algorithm Cares About

- **First 3 seconds decide everything.** Completion rate is the top signal, but the early seconds are weighted heaviest. If they leave early, the rest doesn't matter.
- **Rewatches are gold.** Multiple views from the same person is a strong positive signal.
- **Shares to DMs drive reach.** The share button is more powerful than likes.
- **Niche beats broad.** Since 2025, TikTok rewards creators who serve specific communities (#BusinessTok, #AutomationTok) over generic viral attempts.
- **AI categorisation.** TikTok classifies your content by type — education, humour, story — and matches it to users who engage with that type. Being consistent helps.
- **Production quality is factored in.** Good lighting, clear audio, clean editing all contribute to ranking now.

The testing pattern: your video goes to a small batch (~200-500 people). If they engage, it goes to a bigger batch. Each batch has to perform for the next one to happen. That's why the first 3 seconds make or break everything.

Since 2025: longer videos (30-60+ sec) are getting pushed if retention stays high. It's not just short clips anymore.

### Video Structures

**Quick teach (15-30 sec):**
```
[0-3s] Hook — make a bold claim or call out your audience
[3-25s] Deliver 3-5 points, rapid-fire
[25-30s] CTA or loop point (end where you started)
```

**Story format (30-60 sec):**
```
[0-3s] Drop in mid-story ("So there I was...")
[3-10s] Quick context
[10-45s] Build tension
[45-60s] Payoff or lesson
```

**Myth bust (15-45 sec):**
```
[0-3s] "Stop believing [myth]"
[3-30s] Why it's wrong and what's actually true
[30-45s] What to do instead
```

### Hooks That Stop the Scroll

1. **Call out an identity:** "This is for the [specific group]..." / "Only [role] will get this"
2. **Pattern interrupt:** Start mid-action. No intro. Something unexpected on screen.
3. **Stir the pot:** "I'm going to get hate for this but..." / "[Popular take] is completely wrong."
4. **Show the result first:** Lead with the outcome, then explain how. Before/after but backwards.
5. **Open a gap:** "The thing about [topic] that nobody is willing to say out loud..."

### Things That Help

1. Trending sounds (even at low volume) boost discoverability
2. Captions are non-negotiable — most people watch on mute
3. Loop your endings back to the start for rewatch credit
4. End with a question or hot take to bait comments
5. "Part 1" / "Episode 2" drives follows and series engagement
6. Green screen (you + content behind you) works well for educational content

### Don't Do This

1. Slow intro (if nothing happens in 3 seconds, they're gone)
2. No captions (you lose most of your audience)
3. Over-polished production (raw and real outperforms slick)
4. Ignoring comments (replying is both engagement and content)

## YouTube

### What the Algorithm Cares About

- **Click-through rate (CTR).** How often people click your thumbnail and title when it appears. This determines how much YouTube shows your video.
- **Watch duration.** Both raw minutes watched and percentage of video completed. YouTube wants people watching for a long time.
- **Session time.** If your video leads to more YouTube watching (not leaving the platform), that's a big positive signal.
- **Satisfaction.** YouTube runs viewer surveys and combines them with behaviour patterns to estimate whether people actually valued your content.
- **Channel focus.** Channels that stick to a niche get recommended more than channels that bounce between topics.

Since 2025: stronger personalisation based on device, time of day, individual habits. Old videos get resurfaced when their topic trends. Authority and depth matter more (aligned with Google Search updates).

### Shorts vs Long-Form

**Shorts** are for discovery. 10-35 seconds. Good for gaining subscribers, less valuable for watch time metrics. Think of them as trailers for your channel.

**Long-form** is where the value compounds. 8-12 minutes is the sweet spot for most niches. Search + browse discovery. This is where you build a real audience.

### Short-Form Patterns

**Quick tip:** "[Number]-second [topic] tip" → deliver it → "Subscribe for more"

**Best bit:** Pull the best moment from a long video → make it self-contained → "Full breakdown on my channel"

**Show the result:** Flash the outcome → quick explanation → "Want the full walkthrough?"

### Long-Form Structure

**First 30 sec (Attention):** Hook them. Bold claim, pattern interrupt, or a quick preview of what they'll get. Brief credibility if needed.

**30s - 2 min (Interest):** Why this matters. Who should watch. Set expectations so they commit to the full video.

**Main section (Desire):** Deliver the goods. Break it into clear sections. Use examples, proof, and results. Signpost verbally — "First... Next... Finally..."

**Last 30 sec (Action):** Summarise the key points. Give one clear next step. End screen with subscribe + "Watch this next."

### Titles That Drive Clicks

**Process:** "How I [Result] in [Time]" / "How to [Outcome] (Step-by-Step)"

**List:** "[Number] [Things] That [Outcome]" / "[Number] Mistakes [Role] Make"

**Curiosity:** "Why [Surprising Thing] Is [Counterintuitive Truth]"

**Journey:** "I [Did Thing] for [Time Period]. Here's What Happened."

### Don't Do This

1. Clickbait that doesn't deliver (YouTube correlates CTR with retention — high clicks + low watch time = punishment)
2. No hook in the first 30 seconds (people leave and you lose watch-time signal)
3. Cluttered thumbnails (max 3 elements: face, text, object)
4. Missing cards and end screens (free tools for keeping people on your channel)
