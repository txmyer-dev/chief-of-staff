# ICP Interview Questions

Ask a maximum of 8 from this bank. Prioritise based on what's already known from context. The goal is to surface the customer's world — not the founder's framing of it.

---

## Core Identity (pick 2-3)

1. **Who is your best customer right now?** Not the most common — the best. The one who gets the most value, stays the longest, refers others. Describe them specifically.

2. **What's their situation when they find you?** What just happened? What are they searching for? What's the trigger that makes them look for a solution right now?

3. **What's their job title or role — and what do they actually spend their time doing?** (Job titles lie. "Marketing Manager" might mean "I do everything including taking out the trash.")

4. **How would they describe themselves?** Not how you'd describe them. If they were filling out a form that said "I am a ___", what would they write?

## Pain & Problem (always ask these)

5. **What's the specific problem that drives them to look for a solution?** Push for specificity. Not "they need more leads" — what does the lack of leads actually mean for their day-to-day life? What does it cost them?

6. **What have they already tried that didn't work?** This reveals sophistication level, frustration level, and objections you'll need to overcome.

7. **How do they describe this problem to a friend?** Not in professional language — in the casual, frustrated words they'd use over drinks. This is the language that resonates in copy.

## Decision & Purchase (pick 2-3)

8. **What would make them skeptical of your solution?** What's the first objection? The thing they think but don't say? The reason they almost didn't buy?

9. **What finally pushed them to buy?** Was it a specific event, a deadline, a pain point reaching a threshold? This is the buying trigger.

10. **Who else is involved in the decision?** Is there a boss, a partner, a team that needs to approve? What do THOSE people care about?

11. **Where do they go for advice in your space?** Podcasts, newsletters, communities, specific people they trust? This tells you where to find more of them.

## Outcome & Aspiration (pick 1-2)

12. **What does success look like 6 months after using your product?** What changed? What's their life/business like now? Be specific.

13. **What would they tell a friend about the experience?** Not a testimonial — the real, casual version. "Honestly, [product] was the first thing that actually..."

14. **What do they brag about after getting results?** This reveals the deeper motivation beneath the surface-level problem.

---

## Synthesis Guidance

After collecting answers, map them to the ICP profile sections:

| Question area | Maps to profile section |
|--------------|------------------------|
| Identity questions | Who they are |
| Pain questions | Primary pain + What they've tried |
| Question 7 (friend language) | Their language — this is gold |
| Skepticism (Q8) | Objections |
| Buying trigger (Q9) | Buying triggers |
| Decision-maker (Q10) | Who they are (expanded) |
| Advice sources (Q11) | Where they hang out |
| Outcome questions | Desired outcome |

**Key principle:** When the founder's description of the customer diverges from how the customer would describe themselves, always use the customer's framing. The ICP should read like something the customer would recognise as "someone who really gets me."
