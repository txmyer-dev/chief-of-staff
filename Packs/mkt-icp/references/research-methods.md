# ICP Research Methods

How to extract customer profile data from public sources. The goal is real customer language and patterns — not assumptions.

---

## Source Priority

Research these in order. Stop when you have enough signal (aim for 20+ distinct customer statements across sources).

### 1. Customer Reviews (Highest Value)

Reviews of the product itself or direct competitors on: Amazon, G2, Capterra, Trustpilot, app stores.

**What to extract:**
- How they describe the problem BEFORE using the product
- What they tried before (alternatives, DIY, doing nothing)
- What surprised them (reveals unmet expectations)
- What they wish was different (reveals secondary pains)
- Exact phrases they use repeatedly across reviews

**Pattern to watch for:** The words that appear in 3+ reviews are the customer's natural vocabulary. These belong in the ICP language section.

**Star rating signal:** 3-4 star reviews are the most useful. 5-star reviews are often generic praise. 1-star reviews are often situational frustration. 3-4 star reviews contain nuanced feedback.

### 2. Reddit & Forum Threads

Search for threads where the target audience discusses the problem the product solves.

**Search patterns:**
- "[problem] reddit"
- "[product category] vs [alternative]"
- "best [solution type] for [audience]"
- "frustrated with [current approach]"

**What to extract:**
- How they frame the problem (their words, not industry jargon)
- What solutions they've tried and why they switched
- What they're really optimising for (speed? cost? quality? simplicity?)
- Emotional language — frustration, hope, resignation

**Authenticity check:** Reddit language is usually more honest than review language. People complain more specifically and recommend more genuinely.

### 3. Social Media Comments

Comments on competitor content, industry influencer posts, and relevant hashtags.

**Where to look:**
- LinkedIn posts from thought leaders in the space
- Twitter/X threads about the problem
- YouTube comments on related tutorials or reviews
- Facebook group discussions

**What to extract:**
- Questions people ask (reveal knowledge gaps and pain points)
- Objections they raise in comments
- How they describe their situation
- Who they tag or reference as authorities

### 4. YouTube Comments

Particularly valuable for understanding sophistication level and specific questions.

**Search for:** "[product category] review", "[problem] tutorial", "[competitor] walkthrough"

**What to extract:**
- "I've been looking for something that..." (reveals desired outcome)
- "Does this work for [specific situation]?" (reveals segments)
- "I tried [alternative] and..." (reveals alternatives and frustrations)

### 5. Survey Data / Testimonials

If the user provides these, they're gold — direct customer voice.

**What to extract:**
- Transformation stories (before/after)
- Specific results (numbers, timeframes)
- Decision-making process
- Referral language ("I tell my friends...")

---

## Synthesis Process

After collecting data across sources:

### 1. Pattern Extraction

Group customer statements into themes:
- **Pain patterns** — what problems come up most? What language recurs?
- **Alternative patterns** — what have they tried? What specifically failed?
- **Outcome patterns** — what do they actually want? (Often different from what founders think)
- **Objection patterns** — what makes them hesitate?

### 2. Language Mining

Pull exact phrases that appear across multiple sources. These are the customer's natural vocabulary:
- How they name the problem
- How they describe the desired outcome
- How they describe themselves and their situation
- Emotional words they use (frustrated, overwhelmed, stuck, excited, finally)

### 3. Segment Identification

If the research reveals multiple distinct customer types, note them all but identify the primary:
- **Primary segment:** Largest, most valuable, most clearly defined
- **Secondary segments:** Smaller or less defined — note for future reference

### 4. Assumption Validation

If the founder has already described their customer (Interview mode), compare:
- Where does the founder's description match customer reality? (Reinforces)
- Where does it diverge? (Flags for discussion)
- What did the research reveal that the founder didn't mention? (Enriches)

Flag divergences explicitly: "Your customers describe their problem as [X], but you described it as [Y]. The customer's framing is more likely to resonate in copy."
