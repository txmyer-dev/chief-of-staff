# Brand Voice Build Questions

Read this when running Mode 2 (Build). Ask a maximum of 8 questions.
Prioritise based on what context is already loaded from brand_context/.

---

## Two Approaches

### Approach A: Conversational Interview (Default)
Best for: Quick setup, users who prefer to talk it through.
Use the Question Bank below — pick the most relevant 6-8 questions based on what context you already have.

### Approach B: Guided Questionnaire
Best for: Users who want to think through their voice methodically, especially when no content samples or URLs are available.
Use the Structured Questionnaire below — walk them through it section by section.

If the user mentions having example posts, content they like, or links to reference — combine with Extract mode (Mode 1) for best results.

---

## Approach A: Question Bank

### Identity (pick 2-3)

1. What are 3-5 words that describe your personality — not your brand's positioning, but how you actually show up?
2. What do you stand for? What's your core belief about your industry or topic that most people in your space wouldn't say out loud?
3. What's your background? What experiences shaped how you see things?
4. What makes you genuinely different from others in your space — not just what you offer, but how you think?

### Audience (pick 1-2)

5. Who are you talking to? Be specific — not "entrepreneurs", but what kind, at what stage, with what problem?
6. What tone resonates with your audience? What do they respond to? What would turn them off?

### Positioning (skip if positioning.md is loaded)

7. Are you the expert, the peer, the rebel, the guide, the insider? Where do you sit on approachable ↔ authoritative?

### Aspiration (always ask this one)

8. Name 2-3 people or brands whose voice you admire. What specifically do you like about how they communicate? And what do you explicitly NOT want to sound like?

### Practical (always ask these)

9. Any words or phrases that are signature to you — things you say all the time?
10. Any words or phrases you hate or actively avoid? Things that feel off-brand the moment you read them?
11. How do you feel about: humor? Profanity? Hot takes? Being polarising?

## Approach B: Structured Questionnaire

Walk the user through these sections ONE AT A TIME. Each section has a question and examples to help them think. Capture their answers — these become the raw material for the voice profile.

### Section 1: Your Anti-Corporate Stance
**Question:** "What annoys you most about how business is typically done in your industry?"

Example: "I hate how everyone talks about 'synergy' and 'leveraging opportunities' when they really mean 'let's actually get stuff done.'"

*What this reveals: Their "never" list, anti-patterns, and what makes them different from the crowd.*

### Section 2: Your Problem-Solving Style
**Question:** "When someone comes to you with a problem, how do you naturally respond?"

Give them options to react to:
- "Let's figure this out together" (collaborative)
- "Here's exactly what you need to do" (directive)
- "I've been there too, here's what worked" (experiential)
- "Let me ask you a few questions first" (diagnostic)

*What this reveals: Their relationship stance — guide, teacher, peer, consultant.*

### Section 3: Your Communication Style
**Question:** "In real conversations, are you more..."

- Direct and blunt (cut through the BS)
- Diplomatic but honest (gentle truth-telling)
- Story-driven (explain through examples)
- Question-focused (get people thinking)

*What this reveals: Tone spectrum positioning.*

### Section 4: Your Origin Story
**Question:** "What's the most interesting part of your journey that shaped how you see your industry?"

Examples:
- "I studied engineering but never worked as one"
- "I was the customer service rep who saw what really frustrated clients"
- "I built my business while working corporate and hated the rules"

*What this reveals: Core energy, credibility source, recurring themes.*

### Section 5: Your "Why This Matters" Moment
**Question:** "What drives you personally about the work you do? Not the mission statement. The real reason."

Examples:
- "Freedom to work how I want"
- "Helping people escape the busy work that's killing their passion"
- "Proving there's a better way than what everyone else is doing"

*What this reveals: Authentic motivation that should come through in every piece of content.*

### Section 6: Content Comfort Zone
**Question:** "When creating content, you feel most authentic when you're..."

- Sharing personal stories and lessons
- Breaking down complex topics simply
- Calling out industry problems
- Providing step-by-step solutions

*What this reveals: Content pillars and natural voice mode.*

### Section 7: Natural Language Patterns
**Question:** "Think about how you actually talk. Do you..."

- Use "we" and "us" or "you" and "I"?
- Ask questions or make statements?
- Use technical terms or everyday language?
- Tell stories or stick to facts?

*What this reveals: POV, vocabulary level, rhythm patterns.*

### Section 8: Voice Examples (Optional but Powerful)
**Question:** "Can you share 2-3 links to posts, emails, or content you've written that you felt really represented you? Or paste in some text that sounds like your authentic voice?"

If they provide links: use WebFetch or Firecrawl to extract and analyze the content. Feed into the extraction methodology (see `extraction-guide.md`).

If they paste text: analyze directly for linguistic patterns.

*What this reveals: The gap between how they describe their voice and how they actually write. Real samples always beat self-description.*

## Synthesis Process

Once you have answers (from either approach), construct the voice profile in this order:

**1. Extract the core energy first**

Before writing anything, identify:
- What role does this person naturally play? (Teacher / Challenger / Cheerleader / Straight-shooter / Guide / Insider)
- What's their default energy? (Calm authority / High enthusiasm / Understated confidence / Raw honesty)
- What recurring themes came up that they clearly care about?

This becomes the anchor. Everything else should be consistent with it.

**2. Define the tone spectrum**

Map where they land on each dimension using their answers as evidence:
- Formal ↔ Casual
- Serious ↔ Playful
- Reserved ↔ Bold
- Simple ↔ Sophisticated
- Warm ↔ Direct

For each dimension, note WHY based on their answers. Don't just assign a position without reasoning.

**3. Identify linguistic habits**

This is what makes a voice profile actually useful vs. generic. Look for:
- **Sentence starters:** Do they use conversational connectors? ("So," "And," "But,")
- **Intensifiers:** Do they repeat words for emphasis? ("so, so easy," "really, really")
- **Directive patterns:** Do they guide with "you" statements? ("All you need to do is...")
- **Complexity acknowledgment:** Do they call out hard parts honestly?
- **Personal sharing:** Do they reference their own struggles and discoveries?

These patterns are more valuable than broad tone descriptions. They're what makes writing sound like a specific person vs. a generic brand.

**4. Set vocabulary rules**

From their answers, build:
- Words/phrases to use (signature terms they mentioned, their natural language)
- Words/phrases to avoid (anything they said feels wrong, plus any corporate jargon that contradicts their energy)
- Jargon level (based on who they said their audience is)

**5. Map confidence zones**

Based on their stated expertise and background, assign topics to zones:
- Full authority: their core expertise areas — speak definitively
- Earned perspective: areas with experience but not mastery — use "in my experience"
- Active exploration: areas they're currently figuring out — speak curiously

**6. Write example phrases**

Generate 3-5 phrases in their voice based on everything above. These should feel specific, not generic. Then write 2-3 anti-examples — the same ideas in the wrong voice.

**7. Platform adaptations**

Based on which platforms they mentioned and their audience:
- How does the core voice flex per channel?
- What stays constant, what adjusts?
- Include samples for each platform they'll use

---

## Quick Synthesis Check

Before producing the profile:
- [ ] Does the core energy description feel accurate to who they described?
- [ ] Are the vocabulary rules specific enough to be useful?
- [ ] Did you capture specific linguistic habits (connectors, intensifiers, directive patterns)?
- [ ] Could a ghostwriter pick up this profile and write in their voice without asking more questions?
- [ ] Did you account for any aspirational voice elements they mentioned (who they admire)?
- [ ] Are samples organized by platform/medium?
